In November 2025, Martha Teye attended the British Machine Vision Conference (BMVC) at the Cutler‘s Hall in Sheffield (UK), where she had the opportunity to present her work.<br>
She presented a poster on the work we did with <a href="https://bmvc2025.bmva.org/proceedings/220/">Lidar MOT-DETR</a>. She shared that being able to showcase her research among so many brilliant minds in computer vision was both humbling and energizing.
<br><br>
Throughout the conference, we had the chance to:
<br>• Dive deep into fascinating discussions on vision transformers, multimodal learning, and tracking
<br>• Learn from impressive keynote from Marc Pollefeys (ETH Zurich) on how scene understanding can aid people and robots in interacting with the world more intelligently.
<br>• Experience the inspiring atmosphere that comes from being surrounded by people pushing the boundaries of visual intelligence.
<br><br>
Presenting this work was a milestone not just for the moment itself, but for the journey leading up to it. This work was made possible through Martha's efforts, together with the support and contributions of the labs advisors, collaborators, and colleagues.
